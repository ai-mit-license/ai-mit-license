# Changelog

All notable changes to the AI-MIT License will be documented here.

The format follows [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.0.0/).  
The license version follows a simple `MAJOR.MINOR` scheme — MAJOR for breaking changes to legal obligations, MINOR for clarifications and additions.

---

## [1.0.0] — 2025

### Initial release

**Core structure:**
- Authorship Declaration with three modes: Fully AI-generated / AI-assisted / Human-authored
- Grant of Rights (MIT-compatible, permissive)
- Three conditions: Attribution, Transparency, No Copyright Claim
- AI-specific disclaimer clauses: training data provenance, regulatory uncertainty, jurisdictional limitations

**Translations:**
- English (authoritative)
- Russian (non-authoritative)

**Rationale:**  
No existing open-source license addresses the reality of AI-generated code — the false implication of human authorship, the copyright grey zone for autonomous generation, or the training data provenance problem. AI-MIT fills this gap with the minimum necessary additions to the MIT structure.
